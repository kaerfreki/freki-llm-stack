INFERENCE PLATFORM — OPERATIONS RUNBOOK (v1.4)

Scope. This runbook covers day-2 operations of the self-hosted inference
platform serving open-weights language models to internal applications.

Monitoring. Dashboards track time to first token, sustained generation
rate, and GPU memory occupancy. A warning alert fires when GPU memory
usage stays above 14 GiB for more than ten consecutive minutes; a critical
alert fires when any inference request fails with an out-of-memory error.
Alert fatigue reviews happen monthly.

Model lifecycle. Models are pulled only from the internal registry mirror,
never directly from the public internet. A model stays loaded in GPU
memory for five minutes after the last request it served, after which it
is evicted to make room for other models. Evictions are logged.

Backups. The model volume is backed up nightly at 02:00 UTC with a
fourteen-day retention window. Backup restoration is rehearsed on the
first Tuesday of every month; the March rehearsal surfaced a permissions
regression that was fixed in v1.3 of this runbook.

Change management. The runtime container image is version-pinned. After
the March service outage, all image upgrades require a staging soak of at
least 48 hours and a rollback plan filed in the change ticket before
production rollout.

On-call. The platform is covered by a weekly on-call rotation of three
engineers. Escalation beyond the rotation goes to the infrastructure lead.
